# 03 - Hero, Scope And Evidence Rules

## Objective

Rewrite the opening so the page has a public thesis, clear scope and evidence contract.

## Read first

- `audit/rewrite-blueprint.md` sections 0-1
- `audit/README.md`
- `audit/claim-ledger.md` C01, C55
- `research/README.md`

## Work

1. Edit the hero/lead and early introduction in `index.html`.
2. Keep the central idea: practical taxonomy, not formal ontology.
3. Add a short reader-facing "how to read evidence" section or compact note:
   - definitions vs taxonomy decisions;
   - source evidence vs page inference;
   - evidence strength;
   - living docs/product claims require review dates.
4. Avoid citation clutter. If annotation scaffolding exists, use it for the evidence note.
5. Do not introduce new unsupported claims about completeness, consensus or market adoption.

## Suggested thesis

"This page is a practical map for choosing the smallest agent architecture that works: autonomy, topology, coordination, composition, memory, protocols, safety and evals are separate decisions, not one maturity ladder."

## Acceptance criteria

- A first-time reader understands what the taxonomy is for.
- The page explains that it is not a formal ontology.
- The evidence policy is visible without making the page feel academic.
- No "old source" or "task completion" language remains in the opening.
- `prompts/progress.md` is updated.
